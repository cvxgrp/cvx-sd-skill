**Epigraph for `philosophy.md`:**

> "Instead of specifying it in full detail, you specify it only somewhat; you
> then ride on the dynamics of the system in the direction you want to go."

**ATTRIBUTION — be careful, do NOT cite as a clean Stafford Beer quote.** The
words are **Brian Eno's paraphrase** of an idea from Stafford Beer's *Brain of
the Firm*, from Eno's interview with David Whittaker. Documented in a 2010
academic article (full citation TBD) which itself hedges "apparently
paraphrasing." So the honest status: Eno's crystallization of Beer's concept;
there may be no verbatim Beer source.

**Preferred framing (Option B): lean into the Beer→Eno lineage.** Eno took
Beer's steer-don't-specify cybernetics and made it a *generative method* for
music; this project does the same for generating convex models with AI. The
lineage Beer → Eno → (this work) is itself the point; the epigraph models the
very reframing it argues for.

**Why it's load-bearing (works on multiple levels):**
1. The specification philosophy / tuning hierarchy — under-specify, ride the
   solve/holdout/widget dynamics ([[binding-constraint-hyperparameter-specification]]).
2. Loss-as-prior — a penalty *is* specifying-only-somewhat + a direction.
3. The skill's own design — give the agent substrate + direction, ride its
   capability ([[graceful-degradation-model-capability]]).
4. The collaboration funnel ([[collaboration-funnel-author-contact]]).
Cybernetic lineage (Beer, viable systems, steering-not-controlling) is a real
ancestor of the thesis's do-more-with-less stance.
